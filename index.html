Inspired by the energy of London's music scene comes the Air Max

Pulse. a tough silhouette infused with an unreal sensation of Air.

1987: Air Max 1

Inspired by the open architecture
of Paris’s Centre Pompidou, Nike
Designer Tinker Hatfield cut a small window into
the Air Max 1’s midsole,
making Nike Air visible for the first time.

1990: Air Max 90

With its standout side panel,
the AM 90 was a revolution of its own.
Its original ‘Infrared’ colorway
still creates a sensation with
every reissue.

1995: Air Max 95

Our designers took inspiration from the earth’s layers
for the 95’s sculptured look. This
Air Max shoe was also the first to
add a second visible Nike
Air unit at the front of the foot.
